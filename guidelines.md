# Milestone 1

As part of your project on AI applied to drug discovery, you are required to prepare your Milestone 1 Deliverable. Format is **three pages** Times New Roman 11pts single space including figures, margins 2 cm. References including DOI-URL on extra pages.

To structure your transition from proposal to execution, please divide your report into the following sections, adhering strictly to the page limits for each part:

## Part I: Introduction and Background (1 Page Maximum)

*This section should be a rescoped and compacted version of your Letter of Intent (LOI), incorporating the feedback you received.*

1. **Research Question & Significance:** Concisely state the specific question your project addresses. Explain its importance to AI in drug discovery.

2. **Context & Datasets:** Summarize the current landscape and the specific data that your approach relies on.

## Part II: Proposed Methods and Setup (2 Pages)

*This is the new contribution for this milestone. Detail the concrete computational steps you will take.*

3. **Proposed Methods:** Detail the specific machine learning architectures and algorithms you plan to implement. Whether you are using causal inference frameworks for target identification, representation learning for high-content screening (e.g., Cell Painting), or graph neural networks for property prediction, be highly specific. Answer the question: how is your method innovating in AI for drug discovery or improving upon traditional data science approaches?

4. **Data and Preprocessing:** Describe your explicit data processing pipeline. Detail your dataset splitting strategy (e.g., scaffold splits) to rigorously avoid data leakage.

5. **Information Flow Diagram:** Include a clear flow diagram illustrating the architecture and flow of information in your proposed approach. Explicitly label the specific raw inputs (e.g., SMILES strings, target protein sequences, or raw high-content screening images), the intermediate representation steps, and the expected outputs (e.g., binding affinity scores, generated 3D conformers, or phenotypic classifications).

6. **Experimental Setup & Baselines:** Establish the formal start of your experimental section. Define your primary and secondary evaluation metrics. Lock in the specific baseline models or heuristics you will compare your method against. You do not need to have them implemented for this writeup.

7. **Timeline to Milestone 2:** Provide an actionable timeline detailing the concrete steps to reach your final report. Identify anticipated computational bottlenecks (e.g., cluster queue times) and explicitly state team member responsibilities.
