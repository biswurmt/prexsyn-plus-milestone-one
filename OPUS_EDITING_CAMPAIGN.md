## THE FIVE EDITING PASSES

### **PASS 1: Style Enforcement & Jargon Elimination (3–4 hours)**

**Focus:** Strip the document of style violations and make chemistry/molecular concepts accessible to a mixed audience.

**Specific checks:**
- **Em dashes:** Find and replace EVERY em dash with commas, parentheses, or separate sentences. This is non-negotiable per CLAUDE.md.
- **Anthropomorphism:** No model "learns," "understands," "discovers," or "struggles." Replace with "encodes," "predicts," "optimizes," "maps."
- **Filler words:** Hunt for "arguably," "seems," "tends to," "delve," "tapestry," "multifaceted," "revolutionary," "pivotal," "paradigm-shifting," "beacon," "intrinsic." Delete or replace.
- **Jargon gaps:**
  - ECFP4, Tanimoto, desirability, context radius — all need intuitive explanation on first use.
  - When introducing a modification method (CReM, mmpdb, JT-VAE, etc.), explain the *principle* before the name.
  - Example: Instead of "JT-VAE for latent-space editing," write "JT-VAE encodes molecules into a continuous mathematical space, allowing variants to be generated by perturbing learned representations."
- **Chemistry intuition:** For concepts that the pharmacology/biology side might not know (matched molecular pairs, fragment substitution, context radius, synthesizability constraints), add a one-sentence principle explanation.

**Output:** A document with polished prose, no style violations, and clear explanations accessible to non-specialists.

**Commit message:** `docs: pass 1 - eliminate em dashes, anthropomorphism, jargon; strengthen chemistry intuition`

---

### **PASS 2: Drug Discovery Payoff & Explicit Framing (2–3 hours)**

**Focus:** Make the connection between this benchmarking study and real drug discovery crystal clear. Every section should answer: "Why does this matter for discovering new drugs?"

**Specific checks:**
- **Introduction:** Does it explicitly state why synthesis-aware models are valuable to drug discovery? Does it explain why their training-data boundary is a practical constraint? Does it motivate the post-generation modification idea as a complementary strategy?
- **Methods:** For each method (CReM, mmpdb, JT-VAE, LibINVENT, ReactEA), does the text explain *why* it could help a medicinal chemist or AI-driven discovery workflow?
- **Evaluation metrics:** Do they align with what matters in real drug discovery (synthesizability, property conservation, diversity, cost)? Should they be justified explicitly?
- **Expected outcomes / Scope:** Does the "Scope and Limitations" section articulate practical value? (e.g., "These methods could be integrated into agent-driven pipelines to maximize chemical coverage without retraining the generative model.")
- **Complementarity analysis:** Is it positioned as a natural byproduct of the study (useful for pipeline design) rather than the main contribution?

**Example revision:**
- *Weak:* "We benchmark five methods on their ability to produce synthesizable, property-conserving candidates beyond repeated sampling."
- *Strong:* "We benchmark five methods on their ability to produce synthesizable, property-conserving candidates beyond repeated sampling. In medicinal chemistry and AI-driven discovery workflows, this capability is valuable: modification methods escape the building-block constraints of synthesis-aware models, enabling chemists and algorithms to explore a broader space of bioactive chemistry without retraining the generator."

**Output:** A narrative that ties every technical choice back to drug discovery impact.

**Commit message:** `docs: pass 2 - strengthen drug discovery payoff; make motivation explicit throughout`

### **PASS 3: Quantification & Metric Confidence (2–3 hours)**

**Focus:** Replace all qualitative adjectives with exact numbers, bounds, or removal. State metric choices with full confidence (no hedging in methods section).

**Specific checks:**
- **Unquantified claims:** Replace "better performance" with "expansion factor of 1.4×." Replace "significant diversity" with "mean pairwise Tanimoto of 0.48." Replace "markedly improved" with numbers or delete.
- **Metric definitions:** Ensure every metric (hit rate, expansion factor, chemical diversity, complementarity) is precisely defined in the text, not left to the reader to infer.
- **Threshold choices:** Tanimoto ≥ 0.7, desirability ≥ 0.8, ESPsim (if used) — state these as deliberate, confident choices. No hedging like "we tentatively chose." Hedging belongs in the Limitations section, not Methods.
- **Property conservation:** Is the desirability function (geometric mean of per-descriptor scores) justified clearly? Why geometric mean, not arithmetic or min? Why these tolerance functions?
- **Baseline:** Is repeated PrexSyn sampling at N=1000 justified? At what point does it saturate? Have preliminary saturation curves informed N, or is this a placeholder?

**Example revision:**
- *Weak:* "We report results at multiple thresholds to avoid cutoff artifacts."
- *Strong:* "We report results at Tanimoto thresholds of 0.6, 0.7, and 0.85, spanning stringent substructural conservation requirements. This reveals method performance across degrees of structural similarity to the specification."

**Output:** A methods section that reads as decisive and well-reasoned, with exact metrics and justified choices.

**Commit message:** `docs: pass 3 - replace qualitative adjectives with exact metrics; state threshold choices with confidence`

### **PASS 4: Methodological Rigor & Red Flags (3–4 hours)**

**Focus:** Apply your expertise in molecular generation, modification, and drug discovery to flag and resolve methodological fuzziness, unclarified assumptions, and potential confounds.

**Specific checks (reference CLAUDE.md "Red Flags & Things to Challenge"):**

- **Baseline fairness:** Is repeated PrexSyn sampling a fair comparison? If PrexSyn saturates at diversity after 256 samples, does N=1000 for each method tell us anything new? Suggest: "We pilot saturation curves for PrexSyn and each modification method to ensure N is appropriate."

- **Confounded evaluation:** Using the ChEMBL molecule's 3D conformer for ESPsim scoring couples evaluation to the reference beyond its property values. Is this acceptable? Should ESPsim be supplementary-only, not part of hit classification?

- **Generalization scope:** The text says "retrosynthetic analysis against Enamine building blocks." Clarify: Molecules synthesizable through alternative routes may be missed. Enamine REAL is a specific subset of drug-like chemistry, not all synthetically accessible chemistry. Be precise.

- **Method selection rationale:** ~50 methods were surveyed. Why these five? Is that decision visible in the proposal, or does it seem arbitrary? Suggest weaving in a line like: "Selection was informed by a systematic review of ~50 candidate methods, prioritizing coverage across edit scales (local to global) and paradigms (rule-based to learned)."

- **Complementarity novelty:** The analysis assumes methods may overlap. Is that assumption warranted? If methods are truly independent, the analysis may be less novel. Suggest: "A complementarity analysis identifies redundant vs. complementary methods, revealing whether modification strategies at different edit scales produce overlapping or disjoint hit sets."

- **Seed diversity:** Top-1 seeding (K=1) is clean but limited. Top-K seeding is a natural extension. Should the proposal acknowledge this as a future direction or justify single-seed as sufficient?

- **Synthesizability depth:** AiZynthFinder depth 1 vs. 2? The proposal mentions "retrosynthetic analysis" but doesn't specify depth. If depth 1 is primary, state it. If sensitivity to depth matters, say so.

**Action items:**
- If you find genuine ambiguities, add a footnote or parenthetical comment flagging it. Example: `(NOTE: Should clarify whether AiZynthFinder depth-1 or depth-2 is the primary pipeline. Depth-2 catches more routes but is ~5× slower.)`
- If you find an assumption worth questioning, ask inline. Example: `(QUESTION FOR TEAM: Is N=1000 informed by preliminary saturation analysis, or is this a placeholder?)`
- Do not invent answers. If something is ambiguous, mark it for the team to clarify.

**Output:** A document with flagged ambiguities and expert questions that help the team sharpen their design before Milestone 2.

**Commit message:** `docs: pass 4 - flag methodological ambiguities; add expert questions on baseline fairness, confounds, scope`

### **PASS 5: Narrative Flow & Integrative Polish (2–3 hours)**

**Focus:** Read the document end-to-end as a cohesive story. Smooth transitions, check that ideas build logically, ensure every section reinforces the central claim.

**Specific checks:**
- **Story arc:** Does the introduction set up the problem compellingly? Do methods follow logically? Does the scope/limitations section provide honest closure?
- **Transitions:** Are there explicit connectors between sections? ("We investigate a complementary strategy..." "To test this, we...") Or do sections feel disconnected?
- **Repetition:** Is the same idea stated multiple times in different sections? Consolidate.
- **Length balance:** Are some sections overlong, others too brief? Reweight for proportionality.
- **Final read-through:** Read each paragraph aloud. Does it flow? Does every sentence earn its place?

**Example revision:**
- *Choppy:* "We benchmark five methods. CReM is a fragment-substitution tool. mmpdb is a matched molecular pair tool. JT-VAE is a latent-space editor. LibINVENT decorates scaffolds. ReactEA uses reaction rules."
- *Flowing:* "We evaluate five methods that span the rule-based/learned axis and the local-to-global edit-scale axis (Fig. 1). CReM performs fragment-level mutate, grow, and link operations constrained by chemical validity rules. mmpdb mines transformation rules from real compounds. JT-VAE explores latent space via learned representations. LibINVENT and ReactEA extend the benchmark to scaffold-constrained decoration and synthesis-enforced search."

**Output:** A polished document that reads as a unified, compelling narrative.

**Commit message:** `docs: pass 5 - refine narrative flow; integrate sections; final prose polish`
