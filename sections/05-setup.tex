\section*{Experimental Setup \& Baselines}

\textit{Baseline.} Repeated PrexSyn sampling: $N$ additional draws from the same property specification, subject to identical evaluation. This tests whether adding a modification layer outperforms simply sampling more from the generative model.

\textit{Hit classification.} A variant is a hit if it is (a) synthesizable (AiZynthFinder route found) and (b) property-conserving on both axes below. Thresholds are treated as parameters: results are reported at Tanimoto $\geq$ 0.6, 0.7, and 0.85 (spanning lenient to stringent substructural conservation) and desirability $\geq$ 0.8, so conclusions are not artifacts of a single cutoff.

\textit{Property conservation} is assessed on two complementary axes. Substructural: ECFP4 Tanimoto similarity (the fraction of shared circular substructure features) between variant and specification fingerprint. Physicochemical: per-descriptor deltas (MW $\pm$50 Da, CLogP $\pm$1.0, TPSA $\pm$20 \AA$^2$, HBD $\pm$1, HBA $\pm$2, rotatable bonds $\pm$2) mapped to 0--1 desirability scores via linear tolerance functions and aggregated by geometric mean, so a single badly violated descriptor drives the aggregate score toward zero. These are the same descriptors PrexSyn conditions on, ensuring evaluation matches the generator's abstraction level. 3D shape and electrostatic similarity via ESPsim \cite{bib12} is reported as a supplementary diagnostic, not a hit-classification gate, because it introduces a dependency on the reference molecule's 3D conformer geometry.

\textit{Primary metrics.} (1) Hit rate: fraction of synthesizable variants meeting conservation thresholds (higher rates indicate more efficient use of computational budget). (2) Expansion factor: unique hits from modification / unique hits from baseline, the core measure of marginal value (an expansion factor $>1$ means the method produces viable candidates inaccessible to repeated sampling alone).

\textit{Secondary metrics.} Chemical diversity among hits (1 $-$ mean pairwise Tanimoto), measuring whether a method produces structurally varied candidates or clusters around a single chemotype; wall-clock compute cost per specification; proximity to known bioactive space (Tanimoto to ChEMBL reference molecule, diagnostic only).

\textit{Complementarity.} Pairwise Jaccard overlap (shared hits divided by total unique hits across both methods) between method hit sets; identification of optimal 2- and 3-method combinations by total unique-hit coverage. Low overlap indicates complementary methods; high overlap indicates redundancy.

\textit{Stratification.} All metrics reported per baseline-quality bin to reveal when post-generation modification adds the most marginal value. This directly informs practical pipeline design: if modification helps most when the generator performs poorly, it serves as a rescue strategy; if it helps uniformly, it is a general-purpose expansion tool.
